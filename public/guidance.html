Citizen Safety & Prevention Guidance

1. Before Heavy Rainfall (Prevention)

  • Clean local drains around your property to ensure free flow of rainwater.
  • Secure electrical wiring and ensure meters are at a safe height.
  • Stock up on essential supplies (medicine, dry food, clean water) if living in known low-lying areas.
  • Ensure your vehicle is parked on higher ground.

2. During Waterlogging / Flooding

  • Safety first: Avoid walking or driving through flooded waters. Just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down.
  • Electrical Safety: Do not touch electrical poles, wires, or appliances if you are standing in water.
  • Traffic: Avoid underpasses (like Minto Bridge) during heavy rain; they are high-risk zones.
  • Stay tuned to official weather updates and radio broadcasts.

3. After Water Recedes (Health & Safety)

  • Check for structural damage to your property before entering.
  • Boil water for drinking to prevent water-borne diseases like Cholera and Typhoid.
  • Dispose of any food that came into contact with floodwater.
  • Report any stagnant water to the MCD for anti-larval treatment.
